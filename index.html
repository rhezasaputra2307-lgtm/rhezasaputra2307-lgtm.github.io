About

From
the basement
outward

Title Fight formed in Kingston, Pennsylvania in 2003 — four teenagers playing fast, loud, and emotionally unguarded. What started as a straightforward hardcore outfit slowly transformed into one of the most quietly influential bands of the 2010s.

Over a decade-plus, they resisted every expected trajectory. Rather than chasing trends or consolidating a formula, each record arrived as a genuine step into unfamiliar terrain. By the time Hyperview landed in 2015, they had traded power chords for reverb and velocity for atmosphere — arriving somewhere very few bands manage to reach.

Their influence on the wave of bands that followed is hard to quantify but easy to hear: the crawling guitars, the dual-vocalist interplay, the willingness to let a song breathe. They did it first, and they did it without a roadmap.

"Every record we've made has been a direct reaction to where we were emotionally and musically."

— Ned Russin

The Sound

Shedding
Skins

2011

Shed

Raw, fast, and emotionally exposed. The band at their most purely hardcore — every second charged with urgency.

2012

Floral Green

Sharper melodies, more anthemic choruses. The hardcore core still present but alt-rock instincts breaking through.

2015

Hyperview

Velocity replaced by weight. Slow-burning, reverb-drenched, and almost unrecognizable from where they started.

Start with Shed if you want the raw, fast, cathartic version of this band. Move to Floral Green when you're ready for the melodies to open up. Then let Hyperview take its time with you — it rewards patience, repeat listens, and nights alone.

The band that made Hyperview sounds almost nothing like the band that made Shed. That restlessness — that willingness to shed whatever they'd built — is exactly what made them worth caring about.
