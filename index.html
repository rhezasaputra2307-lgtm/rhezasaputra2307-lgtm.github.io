About

Four Kids From Scranton

Title Fight formed in Kingston, Pennsylvania in 2003, starting as a straightforward hardcore punk band and slowly morphing into one of the most fascinating genre-blending acts of their generation. Their sound evolved from aggressive, anthemic post-hardcore into something murkier, more atmospheric, and entirely their own.

Over the course of three full-length albums and several EPs, they carved a path that bridged the rawness of hardcore with the dreamlike haze of shoegaze — a rare combination executed with genuine emotional weight. Their influence on a whole wave of bands that followed is hard to overstate.

"Every record we've made has been a direct reaction to where we were emotionally and musically."

— Ned Russin

The Sound

Where They Sit

Start with Shed if you want the raw, fast, emotional hardcore. Follow it with Floral Green — sharper riffs, anthemic choruses, still a punk record at heart. Then let Hyperview hit you: slow-burning, reverb-drenched, shoegaze-soaked, and completely unlike anything they'd done before.

Each record sounds like a band shedding a skin. That restlessness is part of what made them so compelling — and why people still talk about them years after they went quiet.
